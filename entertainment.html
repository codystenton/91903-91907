Below are some of our top picks for:

Children

Takapuna Beach Playground

Takapuna Beach Playground is a great free spot for families. It sits right by the beach and has beautiful views of Rangitoto Island. The playground has climbing frames, slides, and swings for different ages. That makes it fun for both younger children and older kids. Parents can relax nearby while the children play. There are also cafés and takeaway shops within walking distance. It is a fun and cheap day out.

Teenagers

Arena Entertainment

Arena Entertainment is a fun indoor place for teenagers. It has arcade games, racing games, and other interactive activities. Entry is usually free. Most games cost about $2 to $5 each. It is a great place to hang out with friends. The bright lights and competitive games make it exciting. It is also a good choice on rainy days or cold weekends.

Tourists

North Head

North Head is a great place for tourists to visit. It is free to enter and has amazing harbour views. Visitors can explore old military tunnels and gun sites. The area is full of history and natural beauty. It is also a great place for photos and short walks. North Head is a simple and low-cost way to enjoy Auckland.

Budget Activities

Saturday/Sunday markets

Weekend markets are a fun and cheap activity in Auckland. Entry is usually free. Visitors can look around stalls selling handmade goods, food, and fresh produce. Meals are often affordable, usually around $5 to $15. Many markets also have live music and a friendly atmosphere. They are a great place to relax with friends or family.

Auckland Museum
(Free for Auckland Residents)

Auckland Museum is one of the city’s best attractions. It has displays about Māori culture, natural history, and New Zealand’s past. For Auckland residents, general entry is free. That makes it a great low-cost activity. The museum also has interactive displays and changing exhibitions. It is both interesting and educational for visitors of all ages..

Glenfield Pools

Glenfield Pools is a fun and low-cost activity on the North Shore. It is a great choice in any weather. Pool entry is free for children and teens aged 16 and under. The centre also has a hydroslide and three diving boards. These make it more exciting than a normal swim. The hydroslide and dive pool cost extra, with casual access priced at $8.60. This gives visitors the choice of a free swim or extra fun for a small price.
